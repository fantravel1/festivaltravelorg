Safety Tips

Stay secure and enjoy every moment with our essential festival and nightlife safety advice.

Festival Safety Tips

Following tried‑and‑tested advice can make your festival experience safer and more enjoyable. Here are our top recommendations for attending festivals on your own or with friends:

  • Introduce yourself to neighbours in the campsite and look out for each other’s tents.
  • Learn the route to toilets, water points and the arena during daylight so you can find your way after dark.
  • Look after yourself by staying hydrated and applying sunscreen even when it’s cloudy.
  • Charge your phone daily and carry pre‑charged battery packs for emergencies.
  • Walk confidently when alone; looking like you know where you’re going can deter unwanted attention.
  • Guard your drinks: never leave them unattended or accept drinks from strangers; scrunchie covers can prevent drink spiking.
  • Chat to people around you in the crowd so someone has your back if things get rough.
  • If you feel uncomfortable, head to the nearest information point or zone hub where staff are available 24/7 to help.
  • Report suspicious behaviour to festival staff – you can do this anonymously if you prefer.
  • Make friends via community groups before the event or volunteer to benefit from secure campsites and support networks.

Nightlife & Party Safety

Whether you’re dancing at a festival after party or exploring nightlife in a new city, these tips help you stay safe while having fun:

  • Share your location and tell your friends where you’re going and with whom; never leave without letting them know.
  • Treat your drink like an accessory: keep it in sight at all times and never finish a drink if you didn’t see it prepared.
  • Know your limits and pace yourself with water between alcoholic drinks.
  • Be a good bystander – speak up and alert security if you see suspicious behaviour.
  • Prevent overdoses by testing substances when possible and carrying Naloxone; seek help from staff if someone is in trouble.
  • Practise safe sex: ensure consent, use protection and get tested regularly.
  • Keep your phone charged and carry a portable charger to stay connected and get home safely.
  • Verify rideshare vehicles by checking licence plates and asking who they’re picking up; don’t put an intoxicated friend in a car alone.
  • Plan your night as a group and stick together; share names if going off with new people.
  • Stay aware, trust your intuition and know where exits and bouncers are; leave if you feel unsafe.
