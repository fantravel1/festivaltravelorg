About FestivalTravel.org

A curated guide to festivals that prioritize safety, diversity and inclusivity for women.

What We Do

FestivalTravel.org is a resource for women looking to attend music festivals safely and confidently. Whether you're traveling solo or with friends, we provide information to help you plan your festival experiences.

We focus on festivals that have good safety records, inclusive atmospheres, and positive reputations within the music community. Our goal is to help you make informed decisions about which events might be right for you.

What You'll Find Here

  • Information about festivals known for their safety and inclusive environments
  • Details about venues, stages, and facilities
  • Accommodation recommendations near festival sites
  • Emergency contact information for each location
  • Accessibility information for venues and facilities
  • Basic safety tips and travel advice

We cover a range of festivals from different genres and locations - from established events like Glastonbury and WOMAD to newer festivals making positive changes in the industry.

Creating Memorable Experiences

We believe that every woman deserves to experience the magic of live music in a space where she feels safe, welcome, and empowered. That's why we carefully curate festivals that go above and beyond to create inclusive environments.

How We Choose Festivals

Our Selection Criteria

We look for festivals that demonstrate a commitment to creating safe, welcoming environments. This includes clear anti-harassment policies, adequate security and medical facilities, good accessibility provisions, positive reputation among attendees, transparent communication about safety measures, and inclusive programming with diverse lineups.

We research each festival's safety measures, read reviews from previous attendees, and look at how organizers handle issues when they arise.

Safety First

While we research festivals carefully, your safety is ultimately your responsibility. Always trust your instincts, stay aware of your surroundings, and don't hesitate to seek help if you need it.

Local Research

We encourage you to do additional research about specific locations, check current travel advisories, and familiarize yourself with local emergency services before traveling.

Community

Consider connecting with other festival-goers through official forums or social media groups. Having a network can enhance both safety and enjoyment of your experience.

Our Approach

Empowering Your Journey

We believe that everyone should be able to enjoy live music in environments where they feel safe and welcome. While we can't guarantee any experience will be perfect, we aim to highlight festivals that are actively working to create positive, inclusive spaces.

This site is a starting point for your research, not the final word. We encourage you to read multiple sources, check official festival websites, research accommodation options, plan transportation in advance, and share your plans with trusted friends or family.

Join Our Community

Festival experiences can be transformative and joyful. Our hope is to provide information that helps you feel more confident in choosing events that align with your comfort level and interests.
